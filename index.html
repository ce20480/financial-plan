4. Pension (SIPP)

Personal pension contributions extend your basic rate band, moving income out of 40% tax. [GOV.UK: Pension tax relief]

How tax relief works

For every GBP 100 you want in your pension:

You pay (net contribution)GBP 80
HMRC adds 20% basic rate relief (automatic)+ GBP 20
In your pension potGBP 100
Higher rate relief (via Self Assessment, on amount above GBP 50,270)+ 20% refund

11. Making Tax Digital (MTD)

Does MTD apply to you? MTD from April 2026 requires >GBP 50,000 qualifying income in 2024/25. Since you weren't self-employed in 2024/25 (qualifying income: GBP 0), MTD for Income Tax likely does NOT apply until April 2028 (based on your 2026/27 income). For 2026/27, a normal Self Assessment return may suffice. Check with HMRC after registering. FreeAgent is still valuable for bookkeeping regardless.

When MTD applies (likely from April 2028): Self-employed earning over GBP 50,000 must submit quarterly digital updates to HMRC using MTD-compatible software.

GOV.UK: MTD for Income Tax | GOV.UK: MTD launch announcement

MTD quarterly submissions are REPORTING only. They do NOT change when you pay tax. You send income and expense data to HMRC quarterly via FreeAgent. Tax payments remain on the Self Assessment schedule (31 January / 31 July). These are two completely separate things.

Soft landing: No penalty points for late quarterly submissions in the first year of MTD. [GOV.UK: MTD for Income Tax]
